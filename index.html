About Us

As part of the 2020-21 University of Houston Chemical Engineering Senior class, our group designed a process that could produce 700 kilo tons of ethylene glycol per annum. We made it as efficient, environmentally-friendly and profitable as possible. The work for this project was done over the course of a complete academic year.

We created the intial design without the use of simulation programs in the first semester. We then optimized that design with the use of Aspen Plus in the following semester. Throughout this course, we leveraged concepts, techniques, and ideas from our chemical engineering curriculum at University of Houston.

Introduction

Ethylene glycol serves many industries ranging from automotive to polyesters. It is one of the most important chemicals in industry today, therefore designing a process to produce it offers plentiful challenges and learning opportunities for the up-and-coming chemical engineer.

As mentioned previously, we had no access to process simulation software for the first half of the project. Therefore, we had to rely on first principles and certain assumptions to generate the inital process design. We used the following knowledge to accomplish this first milestone:

  • Unit Operations
  • Thermodynamics
  • Reaction Engineering
  • Heat Transfer
  • Numerical Methods

Methodology

Listed below are the approaches we took for each major unit operation:
